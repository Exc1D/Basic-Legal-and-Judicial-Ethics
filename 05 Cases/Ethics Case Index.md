# Ethics Case Index

Use this folder for case digests and case-to-doctrine links.

## Practice of law anchors

- [[Cayetano v. Monsod]] — broad definition of practice of law.
- [[People vs. Simplicio Villanueva]] — isolated appearance and public-official context.
- [[Ulep v. The Legal Clinic, Inc.]] — corporate practice ban and commercialized legal services.
- [[Aguirre v. Rana]] — unauthorized practice before oath and signing the Roll.
- [[In Re Petition to Sign in the Roll of Attorneys, Michael Medado]] — failure to sign the Roll and unauthorized practice.
- [[Maderada v. Judge Mediodea]] — self-representation distinguished from law practice for another.
- [[Catu v. Rellosa]] — notarial acts and government lawyer restrictions.

## Admission, good moral character, and Bar integration

- [[In re Argosino, B.M. No. 712]] — good moral character as a condition precedent and a rehabilitable, continuing standard. See [[Admission to the Bar]].
- [[Edillon, A.C. No. 1928, 84 SCRA 554 (August 3, 1978)]] — compulsory IBP integration, dues, and freedom of association. See [[Continuing Requirements for the Practice of Law]].

## Law as a profession, not a business

- [[The Practice of Law as a Profession, Not a Business or Trade]] — doctrinal treatment.
- [[Ulep v. The Legal Clinic, Inc.]] — commercialized legal services and misleading advertising.
- [[Zamora vs. Atty. Gallanosa]] — ambulance chasing, encroachment, and neglect.
- [[Burbe v. Atty. Magulta]] — lawyering as public service; misuse of client funds.

## Attorney-client relationship and fiduciary duty

- [[Burbe v. Atty. Magulta]] — relationship begins even without a formal retainer; trust funds and conversion.
- [[Zamora vs. Atty. Gallanosa]] — consultation and drafting may establish the relationship.
- [[Rosario, Jr. v. De Guzman]] — compensation and relationship issues after long representation.
- [[Yamon-Leach vs. Atty. Astorga]] — misappropriation, falsification, and repeated misconduct.

## Attorney's fees

- [[Rosario, Jr. v. De Guzman]] — ordinary compensation, extraordinary fees, quantum meruit, and written fee arrangements.

## Unauthorized practice of law

- [[Ulep v. The Legal Clinic, Inc.]] — corporate practice ban.
- [[Bonifacio v. Atty. Era and Atty. Bragas]] — suspended lawyer continuing to practice.
- [[Gonzales, Jr. and Concepcion v. Attys. Rivera and Bangoy]] — disbarred lawyer co-signing pleadings.
- [[Arienda v. Monilla]] — non-lawyer court employee preparing legal documents. See [[Appearance of Non-Lawyers]].

## Government and court employees

- [[People vs. Simplicio Villanueva]] — isolated appearance by a city attorney.
- [[Office of the Court Administrator vs. Atty. Misael M. Ladaga]] — judiciary employee needs prior written Supreme Court permission. See [[Public Officials and Private Practice]].
- [[Maderada v. Judge Mediodea]] — self-representation distinguished from representing another.
- [[Arienda v. Monilla]] — integrity of court personnel in private dealings.

## Candor, misconduct, and the Lawyer's Oath

- [[Rufina Luy Lim vs. Atty. Manuel V. Mendoza]] — inconsistent sworn positions and intemperate language.
- [[Yamon-Leach vs. Atty. Astorga]] — forged documents and defiance of Supreme Court directives.
- [[Gonzales, Jr. and Concepcion v. Attys. Rivera and Bangoy]] — insubordination independent of the substantive charge.
- [[Canonoy vs. Atty. Guibone]] — suspension reckoning date and compliance manipulation.

## Discipline and mechanics

- [[Almacen, 31 SCRA 562 (G.R. No. L-27654, February 18, 1970)]] — disciplinary power, sui generis proceedings, and limits of criticism.
- [[Canonoy vs. Atty. Guibone]] — suspension begins on receipt; lifting requires sworn compliance.
- [[Calixto vs. Atty. Baleros]] — constructive receipt and notarial misconduct.
- [[Bonifacio v. Atty. Era and Atty. Bragas]] — practicing during suspension.
- [[Gonzales, Jr. and Concepcion v. Attys. Rivera and Bangoy]] — discipline after disbarment and OBC record.
- [[Rufina Luy Lim vs. Atty. Manuel V. Mendoza]] — recidivism and escalation.
- [[Yamon-Leach vs. Atty. Astorga]] — cumulative liability across multiple cases.

## When the privilege yields

- [[People of the Philippines v. Hon. Bonifacio Sanz Maceda and Avelino T. Javellana]] — detention prisoners cannot practice law.

## Related but distinct

- [[New Sampaguita Builders Construction, Inc. and Spouses Dee vs. Philippine National Bank]] — banking/civil law case; keep only for Justice Panganiban links or separate civil-law references.
