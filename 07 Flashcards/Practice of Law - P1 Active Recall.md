# Practice of Law - P1 Active Recall

Use these after reading the doctrine note and the case digest. Cover the answers first.

## Definition and nature

Q: Is practice of law limited to appearing in court?  
A: No. It includes legal advice, legal drafting, representation, and acts requiring legal knowledge, training, judgment, or skill.

Q: What case is the broad-definition anchor?  
A: [[Cayetano v. Monsod]].

Q: What case is the commercialized-legal-service anchor?  
A: [[Ulep v. The Legal Clinic, Inc.]].

Q: What is the first unauthorized-practice trap?  
A: Thinking that only signing pleadings or appearing in court counts as law practice.

Q: What is the pro se distinction?  
A: A person may represent himself, but representing another's legal interests generally implicates practice of law.

## Privilege, not right

Q: Why is law practice a privilege and not a right?  
A: Because the Supreme Court controls admission and continuing membership, and the privilege depends on continuing qualifications and professional fitness.

Q: Does passing the Bar make a person a lawyer?  
A: No. Oath-taking and signing in the Roll of Attorneys are still required.

Q: What case proves that signing the Roll is indispensable?  
A: [[In Re Petition to Sign in the Roll of Attorneys, Michael Medado]].

Q: What is the [[Aguirre v. Rana]] rule?  
A: A Bar passer who practices before completing oath/Roll requirements commits unauthorized practice and may be denied admission.

Q: Does reacquiring Philippine citizenship automatically restore law practice?  
A: No. A separate petition and compliance with Supreme Court requirements are needed.

## Profession, not business

Q: What does it mean that law practice is a profession, not a business?  
A: The primary purpose is public service and administration of justice; compensation is incidental and regulated by professional responsibility.

Q: Which case best connects profession-not-business with fiduciary duty over client funds?  
A: [[Burbe v. Atty. Magulta]].

Q: Why is [[Ulep v. The Legal Clinic, Inc.]] also a profession-not-business case?  
A: It condemns the commercialization and corporate packaging of legal services.

Q: What is the fee trap?  
A: Treating a lawyer-client money dispute as purely civil when fiduciary duties and discipline may also be involved.

## Supreme Court control

Q: Who controls admission to the Philippine Bar?  
A: The Supreme Court.

Q: What constitutional provision is the anchor?  
A: Article VIII, Section 5(5) of the 1987 Constitution.

Q: What is the [[Pimentel v. Legal Education Board]] distinction?  
A: LEB may regulate legal education within limits, but it cannot control admission to the practice of law in a way that displaces the Supreme Court.

## Admission

Q: Is citizenship a technicality in Bar admission?  
A: No. It is a strict threshold and continuing requirement.

Q: What case proves that passing the Bar does not cure citizenship defects?  
A: [[In Re Application for Admission to the Philippine Bar of Vicente D. Ching]].

Q: Is good moral character only checked before admission?  
A: No. It is a continuing requirement for membership in the Bar.

Q: What case is the continuing-good-moral-character anchor?  
A: [[Garrido v. Attys. Garrido and Valencia]].

## Unauthorized practice

Q: What is unauthorized practice of law?  
A: Performing lawyer-exclusive acts without authority to practice, including by non-lawyers or by lawyers whose authority is suspended or lost.

Q: Can a suspended lawyer practice through another lawyer's signature?  
A: No. That may create liability for both the suspended lawyer and the assisting lawyer.

Q: What cases should be paired for suspended-lawyer practice?  
A: [[Tan, Jr. v. Atty. Gumba]] and [[Bonifacio v. Atty. Era and Atty. Bragas]].

Q: Can holding out a non-lawyer as an attorney be a problem even without actual court appearance?  
A: Yes. See [[Cambaliza v. Cristal-Tenorio]].

Q: What is the cleanest one-sentence exam answer?  
A: Unauthorized practice is not measured only by courtroom appearance; it is measured by whether a person without current authority performed or held himself out as authorized to perform acts reserved to members of the Bar.

## Canon I bridge

Q: How does Canon I connect to Practice of Law?  
A: It regulates the quality and independence of the service lawyers render after they accept the privilege to practice.

Q: What does [[Jacolbia v. Atty. Panganiban]] prove under Canon I?  
A: Independent legal service must still be accessible, efficient, and effective; total inaction and refusal to return client property violate professional duties.

Q: What is merit-based practice?  
A: Reliance on the legal and factual merits of the client's cause, not influence, namedropping, threats, pressure, or bribery.

Q: What is the Sec. 2 versus Sec. 4 distinction under Canon I?  
A: Sec. 2 targets improper influence over the deciding tribunal or officer; Sec. 4 targets causing another government office or officer to interfere in a pending matter.
