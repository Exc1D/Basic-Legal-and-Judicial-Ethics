# Legal Ethics Flashcards

Use short, direct prompts. Avoid paragraph answers.

## Practice of law

Q: Is practice of law limited to court appearances?  
A: No. It includes in-court and out-of-court legal work requiring legal knowledge, training, procedure, or judgment.

Q: What must a bar passer do before practicing law?  
A: Take the lawyer’s oath and sign the Roll of Attorneys.

Q: Is self-representation practice of law?  
A: No. A party representing himself or herself acts as a party, not as counsel for another.

Q: Why is practice of law a privilege?  
A: Because it is granted and regulated by the Supreme Court and subject to continuing professional qualifications.

## Admission to the Bar

Q: Under the amended Rule 138 (2025), what age requirement applies to Bar applicants?  
A: "Legal age," replacing the older fixed "21 years of age" figure.

Q: What happened to the residency requirement for Bar admission?  
A: The amended Section 2 dropped the stand-alone requirement that the applicant be a resident of the Philippines.

Q: What is the current standard for a moral-turpitude disqualification?  
A: The charge must have been decided with finality against the applicant — a merely pending charge no longer automatically disqualifies.

Q: Is CLEP completion still optional for Bar applicants?  
A: No. It is now a mandatory prerequisite to sit the Bar Examinations.

## Continuing requirements

Q: Name the seven continuing requirements for the practice of law.  
A: PTR, IBP good standing, IBP dues, MCLE, good moral character, legal aid (ULAS), and citizenship.

Q: How many MCLE hours are required, and over what period?  
A: 36 hours every three years.

Q: How many pro bono hours does ULAS require per compliance period, and how long is that period?  
A: 60 hours every 36 months.

Q: Is IBP membership optional for an admitted lawyer?  
A: No. It is automatic and compulsory, and does not violate freedom of association.

## Law student practice

Q: What are the two certification levels under the Revised Law Student Practice Rule?  
A: Level 1 (after first-year law courses) and Level 2 (upon enrollment in second semester of third-year law courses).

Q: Who signs pleadings filed on behalf of a CLEP client?  
A: The supervising attorney, on behalf of the legal clinic — not the student.

## Non-lawyers and prohibited proceedings

Q: In which courts may a party be assisted by a mere agent or friend instead of a lawyer?  
A: First-level courts only (MTC/MeTC/MCTC).

Q: May a lawyer ever take part in a Katarungang Pambarangay proceeding?  
A: Yes, but only as a party in his own case, or as non-lawyer next-of-kin assisting a minor or incompetent.

Q: Who is barred from appearing at a small-claims hearing?  
A: Lawyers, unless the lawyer is personally the plaintiff or defendant.

## Public officials and private practice

Q: Are governors and mayors absolutely barred from private practice?  
A: Yes, unlike ordinary Sanggunian members, who face only qualified restrictions.

Q: What exactly does the Senator/Representative constitutional bar prohibit?  
A: Personal appearance as counsel before courts, Electoral Tribunals, or quasi-judicial/administrative bodies, including signing or filing pleadings.

Q: Where is the retired Justice/Judge counsel-restriction found in RA 910?  
A: The proviso to Section 1, not Section 5.
