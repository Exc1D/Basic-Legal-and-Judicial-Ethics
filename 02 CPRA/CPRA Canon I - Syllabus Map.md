# CPRA Canon I - Syllabus Map

Canon I should be studied as **independence under pressure**. Each section asks: what pressure is trying to corrupt the lawyer's judgment or the legal process?

## Doctrine sorting

### Sec. 1 - Independence plus service quality

Doctrine: a lawyer's independence is not abstract. It must produce accessible, efficient, and effective legal service.

Case use: [[Jacolbia v. Atty. Panganiban]] is a neglect-and-non-return case. Use it to show that total inaction violates service duties and that refusal to return funds/documents triggers fiduciary consequences.

### Sec. 2 - Merit-based practice

Doctrine: a lawyer must rely on the merits of the client's cause, not influence-peddling, namedropping, pressure, bribery, or claims of special access.

Case use:

- [[Judge Dumlao, Jr. v. Atty. Camacho]] - namedropping, bribery offer, threats.
- [[Jimenez and Vizconde v. Atty. Verano, Jr.]] - lobbying government officials instead of relying on legal merits.
- [[Vantage Lighting Philippines v. Atty. Diño, Jr.]] - claims that a TRO could be bought and retaliatory suits.

### Sec. 3 - Freedom from improper considerations

Doctrine: do not let fear, favor, money, self-interest, dishonest pressure, or outside influence control professional judgment.

Current status: build as a rule-first note because the syllabus does not assign a clean case here.

### Sec. 4 - Non-interference

Doctrine: do not cause an unrelated government branch, agency, office, or officer to interfere with a pending matter unless law or court authority allows it.

Caution: the syllabus lists [[Olvida v. Gonzales]], but the existing vault correctly treats it as a better fit for procedure/strategy and neglect rather than agency interference.

### Sec. 5 - Procedure and strategy

Doctrine: the client determines objectives; the lawyer controls lawful means, procedure, and strategy. A lawyer cannot blame the client for professional procedural neglect.

Case use: [[Olvida v. Gonzales]].

## Bar traps

1. Do not equate independence with ignoring the client.
2. Do not equate accessible legal service with mandatory free service.
3. Do not treat influence-peddling as harmless marketing.
4. Do not assume every syllabus case is perfectly slotted under the correct CPRA section.
5. Do not let pre-CPRA cases confuse you; many old CPR doctrines map into CPRA duties.

## Recit questions

Q: What is the governing value of Canon I?  
A: Independence of legal judgment in service of the client, the courts, and the administration of justice.

Q: What is merit-based practice?  
A: Lawyering based on the legal and factual merits of the client's cause, not on influence, pressure, bribery, or claimed special access.

Q: How do you distinguish Sec. 2 and Sec. 4?  
A: Sec. 2 targets influence over the deciding tribunal or officer. Sec. 4 targets interference by some other government branch, agency, office, or officer in a pending matter.

Q: Why is [[Olvida v. Gonzales]] useful under Canon I?  
A: It shows that procedure and strategy are the lawyer's professional responsibility; neglect cannot be excused by blaming the client.
