## Current-vault Rule 138 summary

The existing [[Admission to the Bar]] doctrine note treats Rule 138 as amended by A.M. No. 24-10-05-SC and flags the following bar traps:

- legal age replaced the older fixed 21-year age formulation;
- Philippine residency no longer appears as a separate stand-alone requirement in the current summary;
- the moral-turpitude disqualification is framed around a charge decided with finality, not merely filed or pending;
- CLEP completion is now treated as a Bar eligibility requirement;
- passing the Bar is not yet authority to practice unless oath and Roll are complete.

## Doctrine links

| Rule 138 issue | Doctrine answer |
|---|---|
| Who may practice law? | Members of the Bar in good standing, subject to limited statutory/rule exceptions. |
| Is Bar passage enough? | No. Oath plus Roll are indispensable before practice. |
| Is citizenship checked only once? | No. Citizenship is a threshold and continuing condition. |
| Is good moral character only for admission? | No. It is a continuing requirement. |
| Can non-lawyers appear? | Only in limited settings allowed by rule or law; appearance is not equivalent to full practice of law. |

## Recall questions

Q: Which Rule 138 concepts should be memorized first?  
A: Admission qualifications, oath-taking, signing the Roll, and limited non-lawyer appearances.

Q: What is the bar trap in oath and Roll cases?  
A: Treating Bar passage, oath, and signing in the Roll as interchangeable. They are distinct steps.

Q: Why is Rule 138 central to Basic Legal and Judicial Ethics?  
A: It defines entry into the profession, the authority to practice, and the limits on who may appear or act as counsel.
