
**Full Case Citation:** A.C. No. 12353, February 6, 2024

**Ponente:** Per Curiam

**Subject:** Legal and Judicial Ethics — Lawyer's Oath; Falsification of Court Documents; Disbarment

> **Gist:** A lawyer who participates in falsifying court documents — here, fabricating a judgment and certificate of finality for an annulment case that was never actually decided — directly contradicts the Lawyer's Oath and warrants disbarment.

## FACTS

Melody H. Santos sought help obtaining a declaration of nullity of her marriage. She was introduced to Atty. Emilio S. Paña, Jr., who, together with an intermediary, promised to secure a decree of nullity within six months, including supporting documents from the civil registry, for a fee of PHP 280,000.00. Atty. Paña eventually handed Santos a Judgment purportedly issued by a named RTC judge, along with a Certificate of Finality. Both documents turned out to be fraudulent — no such judgment had ever been rendered by the court in question.

## ISSUES

Whether a lawyer's participation in producing and delivering falsified court documents to a client constitutes a violation of the Lawyer's Oath and the CPRA warranting disbarment.

## RULING

The SC disbarred Atty. Paña. The Court held that a lawyer's participation in falsifying court documents is a reprehensible act directly contradictory to the oath taken upon admission to the Bar, and demonstrates unworthiness to continue in the practice of law. Handing a client fabricated proof of a favorable judgment that was never rendered is not a procedural lapse or mere negligence — it is an affirmative act of deceit against both the client and the integrity of the courts, striking at the heart of the Lawyer's Oath's promise to do no falsehood and to delay no person for money or malice.

## DOCTRINE

**A lawyer's participation in the falsification of court documents is directly contrary to the Lawyer's Oath and constitutes gross misconduct warranting disbarment**, regardless of whether the lawyer personally forged the documents or merely obtained and passed them on to the client as genuine.

## NOTES

The Lawyer's Oath is not a ceremonial, one-time recitation — it is the standing ethical text every disciplinary decision measures a lawyer against, even where, as here, the specific misconduct also violates named CPRA canons. Pair with [[Pante v. Atty. Tebelin]] for another 2020s disbarment expressly framed as a Lawyer's Oath violation, and with [[In Re Petition to Sign in the Roll of Attorneys, Michael Medado|Medado]] for the point that the oath's obligations begin only once validly taken and the Roll is signed — but bind absolutely once they do.
