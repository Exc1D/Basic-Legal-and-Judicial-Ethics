**Subject:** Legal and Judicial Ethics — Government Lawyers Separated from Service; One-Year Post-Employment Restriction; RA 6713, Sec. 7(b)(2)

> **Gist:** A former Clerk of Court who resigns and, within one year of separation, appears as private counsel in cases before her own former court branch violates RA 6713's one-year post-employment restriction, regardless of the more permissive reading she gave the statute.

## FACTS

Atty. Karen M. Silverio-Buffe resigned as Clerk of Court VI of RTC Branch 81, Romblon, Romblon, effective February 1, 2008. Within one year of her resignation, she engaged in private practice by appearing as counsel in several cases pending before the very same branch where she had served. She raised the question — framed as a query to the Court rather than a defense to a completed complaint — of whether Section 7(b)(2) of RA 6713 (the Code of Conduct and Ethical Standards for Public Officials and Employees) truly barred her from doing so, arguing that the provision's language on avoiding conflicts of interest gave incumbent and separated employees comparably permissive treatment.

## ISSUES

Whether a former court employee's appearance as private counsel before her own former court branch, within one year of separation from service, violates RA 6713's post-employment restriction, and what disciplinary consequence follows.

## RULING

The Court found Atty. Silverio-Buffe guilty of professional misconduct, in violation of Rule 1.01, Canon 1 (unlawful, dishonest, or deceitful conduct) and Canon 7 (upholding the integrity of the profession) of the then-governing Code of Professional Responsibility, and imposed a fine with a stern warning. The Court rejected her reading of Section 7(b)(2): the one-year restriction on a separated government employee practicing before their former office is not merely a conflict-of-interest guideline to be weighed case by case — it is a categorical, time-bound prohibition. Appearing as counsel in cases pending before the exact branch where she had so recently served as an officer of the court was precisely the situation the restriction exists to prevent, given her intimate, insider familiarity with that branch's records, personnel, and processes.

## DOCTRINE

**Under RA 6713, Sec. 7(b)(2), a former government official or employee may not practice their profession in connection with matters before their former office for one year after separation from service.** For a former court employee, this means a categorical bar on appearing as counsel before the specific court branch or office where the employee served, regardless of whether the employee believes no actual conflict of interest exists in a particular case.

## NOTES

This case fills the gap between two rules that, read together, are easy to conflate: Rule 138, Sec. 35 absolutely bars *current* judges and superior-court/OSG officials from private practice (see [[Public Officials and Private Practice]]), while RA 6713, Sec. 7(b)(2) separately restricts *former* officials and employees from practicing before their own former office for a full year after leaving. The Silverio-Buffe query shows the Court reading the post-employment restriction strictly, not as a case-by-case conflict-of-interest inquiry the former employee can talk their way around.
