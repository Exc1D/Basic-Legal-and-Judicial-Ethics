
**Full Case Citation:** G.R. No. 176530, June 16, 2009

**Ponente:** Nachura, J. (Third Division)

**Subject:** Legal and Judicial Ethics — Presumption of a Lawyer's Continuing Authority to Represent a Client; Ratification of Unauthorized Appearance

> **Gist:** A lawyer who appears for a client before the trial court is presumed, under Rule 138, Sec. 22, to continue representing that client on appeal absent a formal withdrawal — and even an appearance the client never expressly authorized may be validated by the client's own silence or acquiescence.

## FACTS

Respondents Dr. Nicasio Gutierrez, Josefa Gutierrez de Mendoza, and Elena Garcia, through their counsel Atty. Adriano Magbitang, sued spouses Constante and Zenaida Agbulos for nullity of a deed of sale, cancellation of title, and reconveyance of inherited land. The trial court dismissed the complaint for lack of jurisdiction, reasoning the dispute was agrarian and belonged before the DARAB rather than the RTC. Atty. Magbitang filed a notice of appeal on the respondents' behalf. One of the respondents, Elena Garcia, then wrote the trial court stating that she and her co-plaintiffs were surprised to learn an appeal had been filed, since there had been no formal agreement with Atty. Magbitang to pursue one — one of the plaintiffs was still abroad at the time. The petitioners argued on review that the appeal should have been dismissed outright because Atty. Magbitang filed the notice of appeal without his clients' knowledge or consent.

## ISSUES

Whether a lawyer's filing of a notice of appeal, without express prior confirmation from the client, is invalid for want of authority, and whether the underlying dispute was properly within the jurisdiction of the regular courts rather than the DARAB.

## RULING

The Court of Appeals, affirmed by the Supreme Court, held the appeal validly taken. Under Rule 138, Sec. 22, an attorney who appears *de parte* in a case before a lower court is **presumed to continue representing that client on appeal**, unless the attorney files a formal petition withdrawing the appearance in the appellate court. Elena Garcia's letter did not actually withdraw Atty. Magbitang's authority — it merely noted that no formal agreement to appeal yet existed. In any event, an attorney's unauthorized appearance may be **ratified** by the client, expressly or impliedly, and ratification **retroacts** to validate the lawyer's actions from the date of the original appearance; the respondents' silence and lack of any objection once the appeal reached the Court of Appeals amounted to an implied ratification. The Court also affirmed that a lawyer's act of filing a notice of appeal without waiting for express client instruction was, if anything, commendable diligence consistent with the duty not to neglect a client's cause. On the jurisdictional question, the Court agreed the dispute was purely civil — seeking nullification of a forged deed and reconveyance — with no tenancy relationship pleaded between the parties, and therefore properly within the RTC's jurisdiction rather than the DARAB's.

## DOCTRINE

**A lawyer's appearance for a client before the trial court carries a statutory presumption of continuing authority through appeal, under Rule 138, Sec. 22, until the lawyer formally withdraws.** An appearance that turns out to be technically unauthorized may still be validated by the client's express or implied ratification — including mere silence or acquiescence — which retroacts to the lawyer's original appearance.

## NOTES

This case is a useful counterweight to the vault's usual "unauthorized practice" cases: here, the question was not whether a *non-lawyer* held out as authorized, but whether a *lawyer's own client* had authorized a particular act of representation — and the Court resolved the doubt in favor of the presumption of continuing authority, reinforced by the lawyer's independent duty of diligence not to let a deadline lapse while awaiting explicit instructions.
