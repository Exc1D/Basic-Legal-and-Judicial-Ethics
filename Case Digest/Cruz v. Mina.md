
**Full Case Citation:** G.R. No. 154207, April 27, 2007

**Ponente:** Austria-Martinez, J.

**Subject:** Legal and Judicial Ethics — Law Student Practice; Non-Lawyer Appearance; Rule 138, Section 34

> **Gist:** A law student may appear before an inferior court as an agent or friend of a party under Section 34, Rule 138 — a track that requires no law-student-practice certification and no supervising lawyer, entirely distinct from and independent of Rule 138-A's Law Student Practice Rule.

## FACTS

Ferdinand A. Cruz, then a third-year law student, sought to appear as private prosecutor for his father, the complaining witness in a grave threats case pending before the Metropolitan Trial Court of Pasay City, relying on his father's written authority and the public prosecutor's prior conformity. The MeTC denied his appearance, reasoning that Circular No. 19 and the Law Student Practice Rule (Rule 138-A) required him to be properly certified and supervised by a member of the Bar before he could appear in any capacity, and that he had not complied with those requirements.

## ISSUES

Whether a law student who does not hold Level 1 or Level 2 certification under Rule 138-A may nonetheless appear before an inferior court as a non-lawyer agent or friend of a party, under a wholly separate rule.

## RULING

The SC ruled in Cruz's favor. Section 34, Rule 138 expressly allows a party before an inferior court (MTC/MeTC/MCTC level) to conduct his own litigation personally, or with the aid of an agent or friend appointed for that purpose — a provision entirely independent of Rule 138-A. Because Cruz was appearing as his father's authorized agent, with the public prosecutor's conformity and the party's written authority, and not by virtue of any law-student certification, the Rule 138-A supervision requirement simply did not apply to him. The Court reaffirmed its earlier ruling in *Cantimbuhan v. Judge Cruz, Jr.*, which permits exactly this kind of lay appearance in inferior courts.

## DOCTRINE

**Section 34, Rule 138 and Rule 138-A (the Law Student Practice Rule) are two separate, non-overlapping tracks.** A law student may appear in an inferior court as a mere agent or friend of a party, without Level 1/2 certification and without a supervising lawyer, so long as the appearance is grounded on the party's own authority under Section 34 rather than on the law student's status as such.

## NOTES

This is the source of a recurring bar trap: examinees assume any law student appearing in court must be operating under Rule 138-A supervision. Cruz v. Mina shows a law student can validly appear on an entirely different, older, and more general basis — the same "agent or friend" provision available to any lay person under [[Appearance of Non-Lawyers]] — provided the appearance stays within a first-level court.
