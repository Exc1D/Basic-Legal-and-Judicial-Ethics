## Start here next week

### Day 1 — Definition and nature of practice of law

Read in this order:

1. [[Practice of Law]]
2. [[Cayetano v. Monsod]]
3. [[People vs. Simplicio Villanueva]]
4. [[Ulep v. The Legal Clinic, Inc.]]
5. [[Arienda v. Monilla]]
6. [[Maderada v. Judge Mediodea]]
7. [[Office of the Court Administrator vs. Atty. Misael M. Ladaga]]
8. [[People of the Philippines v. Hon. Bonifacio Sanz Maceda and Avelino T. Javellana]]

Doctrine to extract: **practice of law is not limited to litigation; it includes legal judgment applied to a person, cause, matter, or engagement, but some restrictions still use habituality, compensation, and representation of another as limiting tests.**

### Day 2 — Privilege, profession, and admission

Read in this order:

1. [[The Practice of Law as "A Mere Privilege, Not a Right" in Philippine Legal Ethics|Practice of Law as a Mere Privilege, Not a Right]]
2. [[The Practice of Law as a Profession, Not a Business or Trade]]
3. [[Burbe v. Atty. Magulta]]
4. [[Rosario, Jr. v. De Guzman]]
5. [[Admission to the Bar]]
6. [[Pimentel v. Legal Education Board]]
7. [[In Re Application for Admission to the Philippine Bar of Vicente D. Ching]]
8. [[In Re Petition to Re-acquire the Privilege to Practice Law, B.M. No. 2112]]
9. [[Garrido v. Attys. Garrido and Valencia]]
10. [[In Re Petition to Sign in the Roll of Attorneys, Michael Medado]]

Doctrine to extract: **the Supreme Court controls admission and continuing membership; passing the Bar is not enough; citizenship, good moral character, oath, and signing the Roll matter separately.**

### Day 3 — Who may practice and unauthorized practice

Read in this order:

1. [[Who May Practice Law]]
2. [[Law Student Practice Rule]]
3. [[Appearance of Non-Lawyers]]
4. [[Public Officials and Private Practice]]
5. [[Unauthorized Practice of Law]]
6. [[Cruz v. Mina]]
7. [[Zeta v. Malinao]]
8. [[Cambaliza v. Cristal-Tenorio]]
9. [[Bonifacio v. Atty. Era and Atty. Bragas]]
10. [[Tan, Jr. v. Atty. Gumba]]

Doctrine to extract: **only members in good standing may practice as lawyers, subject to limited exceptions for self-representation, law student practice, certain non-lawyer appearances, and special tribunals. A lawyer may not directly or indirectly assist unauthorized practice.**

### Day 4 — CPRA Canon I: Independence

Read in this order:

1. [[CPRA Canon I - Quoted Text]]
2. [[Canon I - Independence]]
3. [[Merit-Based Practice]]
4. [[Freedom from Improper Considerations and External Influences]]
5. [[Lawyer's Duty and Discretion in Procedure and Strategy]]
6. [[Jacolbia v. Atty. Panganiban]]
7. [[Judge Dumlao, Jr. v. Atty. Camacho]]
8. [[Jimenez and Vizconde v. Atty. Verano, Jr.]]
9. [[Vantage Lighting Philippines v. Atty. Diño, Jr.]]
10. [[Olvida v. Gonzales]]

Doctrine to extract: **independence protects the lawyer's judgment from pressure, influence-peddling, dishonest motives, improper interference, and client micromanagement of procedure.**
