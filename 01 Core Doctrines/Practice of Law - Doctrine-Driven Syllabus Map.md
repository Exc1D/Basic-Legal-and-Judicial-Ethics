# Practice of Law - Doctrine-Driven Syllabus Map

This map adapts the syllabus to the vault's existing doctrine-first structure. Cases should be learned because they prove a doctrine, not because they appear in a list.

## How to use this map

For every syllabus item, answer four questions:

1. What is the controlling rule or provision?
2. What doctrine does the case prove?
3. What fact pattern triggers the doctrine?
4. What bar trap or recit trap follows from it?

## Minimum recit answers to prepare

Q: What is the practice of law?  
A: It is not limited to court appearances; it includes acts requiring legal knowledge, judgment, or skill, such as legal advice, drafting legal instruments, and representation of another's legal interests.

Q: Why is practice of law a privilege and not a right?  
A: Because admission and continuing membership depend on the Supreme Court's control and on continuing qualifications such as good moral character, oath, Roll, citizenship, and compliance with professional obligations.

Q: What is the first trap in unauthorized practice?  
A: Thinking that only courtroom appearance counts. Out-of-court legal services, legal-document preparation, or commercial legal-service schemes may also constitute practice.
