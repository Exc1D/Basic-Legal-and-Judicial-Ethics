# Practice of Law - P1 Case Comparison Table

Use this for bar-style memory and recitation. The goal is not to memorize facts mechanically; the goal is to know what doctrine each case proves.

## Definition and nature

| Case | Core fact pattern | Doctrine use | Trap |
|---|---|---|---|
| [[Cayetano v. Monsod]] | Broad professional work involving legal knowledge and judgment | Practice of law is not limited to litigation | Do not define practice as only court appearance |
| [[Ulep v. The Legal Clinic, Inc.]] | Commercial legal clinic / legal service advertising | Practice is personal to lawyers; legal services cannot be commercialized through a corporate scheme | Do not treat legal advertising like ordinary business advertising |
| [[People vs. Simplicio Villanueva]] | Limited appearance by government lawyer | Not every isolated appearance equals general private practice | Distinguish isolated appearance from holding oneself out as counsel generally |
| [[Maderada v. Judge Mediodea]] | Self-representation / non-lawyer issue | A person may represent himself; practice of law generally concerns representation of another | Do not confuse pro se appearance with law practice for another |

## Privilege, oath, and Roll

| Case | Core fact pattern | Doctrine use | Trap |
|---|---|---|---|
| [[Aguirre v. Rana]] | Bar passer appeared as counsel before oath and Roll | Passing the Bar is not enough; oath and Roll are indispensable | Do not call a Bar passer a lawyer yet |
| [[In Re Petition to Sign in the Roll of Attorneys, Michael Medado]] | Long period of practice without signing the Roll | The Roll is not a technicality; failure to sign affects authority to practice | Honest mistake may mitigate, but does not erase the requirement |
| [[In Re Petition to Re-acquire the Privilege to Practice Law, B.M. No. 2112]] | Former Filipino lawyer reacquired citizenship | Reacquiring citizenship does not automatically restore the privilege to practice | Citizenship restoration and practice restoration are different steps |

## Profession, not business

| Case | Core fact pattern | Doctrine use | Trap |
|---|---|---|---|
| [[Burbe v. Atty. Magulta]] | Lawyer received money and failed fiduciary duties | Law practice is a profession imbued with public service; client funds create fiduciary duties | Do not reduce the case to a simple debt/fee dispute |
| [[Rosario, Jr. v. De Guzman]] | Fee and representation dispute | Attorney's fees must remain consistent with professional responsibility | Do not treat compensation as the central object of law practice |
| [[Ulep v. The Legal Clinic, Inc.]] | Commercialization of legal services | Profession cannot be converted into ordinary business trade | Advertising can become unauthorized/corporate practice |

## Admission and qualifications

| Case | Core fact pattern | Doctrine use | Trap |
|---|---|---|---|
| [[Pimentel v. Legal Education Board]] | Challenge to LEB's PhiLSAT and legal education regulations | Supreme Court controls admission; LEB regulates legal education within limits | Do not say Congress or LEB controls admission to the Bar |
| [[In Re Application for Admission to the Philippine Bar of Vicente D. Ching]] | Citizenship election made too late | Citizenship is a strict admission requirement | Passing the Bar does not cure defective citizenship |
| [[Garrido v. Attys. Garrido and Valencia]] | Immorality / bigamous relationship | Good moral character is continuing | Time elapsed does not automatically cleanse misconduct |

## Unauthorized practice

| Case | Core fact pattern | Doctrine use | Trap |
|---|---|---|---|
| [[Zeta v. Malinao]] | Non-lawyer court employee represented litigants for pay | Unauthorized practice plus court personnel misconduct | Non-lawyer legal work for pay is dangerous even outside formal pleadings |
| [[Cambaliza v. Cristal-Tenorio]] | Non-lawyer held out as attorney through law office materials | Holding out as a lawyer is itself dangerous | Actual court appearance is not always required |
| [[Tan, Jr. v. Atty. Gumba]] | Lawyer practiced during suspension | Practice during suspension is a new offense | Suspension is not symbolic |
| [[Bonifacio v. Atty. Era and Atty. Bragas]] | Suspended lawyer continued practice; another lawyer assisted | Active assistance in unauthorized practice creates liability | A lawyer cannot lend his name to sanitize unauthorized practice |
| [[Gonzales, Jr. and Concepcion v. Attys. Rivera and Bangoy]] | Disbarred lawyer / co-signing pleadings | CPRA-era treatment of disbarred-lawyer participation | Passive vs active assistance matters |

## One-line memory ladder

1. [[Cayetano v. Monsod]] - broad definition.
2. [[Ulep v. The Legal Clinic, Inc.]] - no corporate/commercial law practice.
3. [[Burbe v. Atty. Magulta]] - profession and fiduciary trust.
4. [[Pimentel v. Legal Education Board]] - Supreme Court controls admission.
5. [[Aguirre v. Rana]] / [[In Re Petition to Sign in the Roll of Attorneys, Michael Medado]] - oath and Roll matter.
6. [[Tan, Jr. v. Atty. Gumba]] / [[Bonifacio v. Atty. Era and Atty. Bragas]] - no practice during suspension.
