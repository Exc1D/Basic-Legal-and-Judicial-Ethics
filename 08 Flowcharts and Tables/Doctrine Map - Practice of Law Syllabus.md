# Doctrine Map - Practice of Law Syllabus

Use this as the map between **rule/provision → doctrine → case → bar trap** for the first part of the syllabus.

## Core doctrine grid

| Doctrine | Rule / provision anchor | P1 case anchors | Exam-ready proposition | Bar trap |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Practice of law is broader than litigation | [[Rule 138 - Attorneys and Admission to Bar]]; [[Practice of Law]] | [[Cayetano v. Monsod]], [[Ulep v. The Legal Clinic, Inc.]] | Legal advice, drafting, negotiation, documentation, and legal judgment outside court may be practice of law. | Do not define practice as court appearance only. |
| Habituality and compensation can limit the inquiry | [[Practice of Law]] | [[People vs. Simplicio Villanueva]] | A single isolated, unpaid, authorized appearance may not amount to private practice. | Do not use *Villanueva* to erase *Monsod*; they answer different questions. |
| Self-representation is not practice for another | [[Appearance of Non-Lawyers]]; Rule 138, Sec. 34 | [[Maderada v. Judge Mediodea]], [[Cruz v. Mina]] | A party may appear personally, but representing another is different. | A non-lawyer's own case is not the same as another person's case. |
| Court/government employees need authority | [[Public Officials and Private Practice]] | [[Office of the Court Administrator vs. Atty. Misael M. Ladaga]], [[Arienda v. Monilla]], [[Ziga v. Judge Arejola]] | Even where an act is isolated or pro bono, a court employee may still need written Supreme Court permission. | Do not confuse “not private practice” with “authorized conduct.” |
| Practice of law is a privilege | [[The Practice of Law as "A Mere Privilege, Not a Right" in Philippine Legal Ethics|Practice as Mere Privilege]] | [[People of the Philippines v. Hon. Bonifacio Sanz Maceda and Avelino T. Javellana]], [[In Re Petition to Sign in the Roll of Attorneys, Michael Medado]] | The privilege requires current authority, freedom to perform duties, and continuing fitness. | Passing the Bar, old admission, or client authorization cannot cure missing authority. |
| Law is a profession, not a business | [[The Practice of Law as a Profession, Not a Business or Trade]] | [[Ulep v. The Legal Clinic, Inc.]], [[Burbe v. Atty. Magulta]], [[Rosario, Jr. v. De Guzman]] | Lawyering is public service and fiduciary work, not commercial salesmanship. | Attorney's fees are lawful, but commercialization and solicitation are not. |
| Admission is under Supreme Court control | [[Admission to the Bar]] | [[Pimentel v. Legal Education Board]] | LEB may regulate legal education, but the Court controls admission to practice. | Do not treat the LEB as co-equal with the Court on bar admission. |
| Citizenship is a threshold and continuing requirement | [[Admission to the Bar]]; [[Continuing Requirements for the Practice of Law]] | [[In Re Application for Admission to the Philippine Bar of Vicente D. Ching]], [[In Re Petition to Re-acquire the Privilege to Practice Law, B.M. | Good moral character is continuing | [[Admission to the Bar]] | [[Garrido v. Attys. Garrido and Valencia]], [[In re Argosino, B.M. No. 712]] | Fitness is checked at admission and throughout the lawyer's career. | Old misconduct can still matter if it shows present unfitness. |
| Oath and Roll are indispensable | [[Revised Lawyer's Oath]]; [[Signing of the Roll of Attorneys]] | [[In Re Petition to Sign in the Roll of Attorneys, Michael Medado]], [[Aguirre v. Rana]] | A bar passer is not yet authorized to practice until oath and Roll requirements are complete. | Do not call a successful examinee a full lawyer too early. |
| Unauthorized practice has two shapes | [[Unauthorized Practice of Law]] | [[Zeta v. Malinao]], [[Cambaliza v. Cristal-Tenorio]], [[Bonifacio v. Atty. Era and Atty. Bragas]], [[Tan, Jr. v. Atty. Gumba]] | It covers non-lawyers doing lawyer-only acts and lawyers practicing without current authority. | A suspended lawyer is not safer because they were once admitted. |
| Lawyer may not assist unauthorized practice | CPRA Canon II, Sec. 35; old Canon 9 case law | [[Bonifacio v. Atty. Era and Atty. Bragas]], [[Gonzales, Jr. and Concepcion v. Attys. Rivera and Bangoy]] | Active help to a suspended/disbarred/non-lawyer actor can trigger discipline. | Passive/ministerial connection is not always active assistance. |
| Canon I protects lawyer independence | [[CPRA Canon I - Quoted Text]]; [[Canon I - Independence]] | [[Jacolbia v. Atty. Panganiban]], [[Judge Dumlao, Jr. v. Atty. ## Decision tree: does the act count as practice of law?

```text
1. Is the person applying law, legal principles, legal procedure, or legal judgment?
   ├─ No → Usually not practice of law.
   └─ Yes → go to 2.

2. Is the person doing it for their own cause?
   ├─ Yes → Self-representation; not practice for another, but other rules may apply.
   └─ No → go to 3.

3. Is the person a member of the Bar in good standing with current authority?
   ├─ Yes → practice may be allowed unless prohibited by office/status/conflict.
   └─ No → possible unauthorized practice.

4. Is the person suspended, disbarred, not yet sworn, not on the Roll, detained, or otherwise disqualified?
   ├─ Yes → no current authority; analyze unauthorized practice.
   └─ No → go to 5.

5. Is the actor a corporation, court employee, government lawyer, judge, law student, or foreign lawyer?
   ├─ Yes → apply the special rule/exception.
   └─ No → default rule: members in good standing may practice.
```

## Recit attack formula

When asked about any case under **Practice of Law**, answer in this order:

1. Identify the actor: lawyer, non-lawyer, bar passer, court employee, government lawyer, corporation, law student, detained person.
2. Identify the act: appearance, drafting, advice, negotiation, document preparation, client funds, advertising, or assistance.
3. Identify the authority problem: no license, no current authority, office prohibition, no written permission, suspended/disbarred status, or corporate shield.
4. State the doctrine.
5. Apply the case as the example.
