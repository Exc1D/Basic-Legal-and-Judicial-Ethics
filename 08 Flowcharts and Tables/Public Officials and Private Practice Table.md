# Public Officials and Private Practice Table

Exam-facing summary of [[Public Officials and Private Practice]]. Sort every fact pattern into one of three buckets before answering: **absolutely prohibited**, **qualified**, or **retired-official restriction**.

## Absolutely prohibited (no private practice while in office)

| Official | Legal basis |
|---|---|
| President, Vice-President, Cabinet members, their deputies and assistants | Constitution, Art. VII, Sec. 13 |
| Members of the Constitutional Commissions (COMELEC, COA, CSC) | Constitution, Art. IX-A, Sec. 2 |
| Ombudsman and Deputy Ombudsmen | Constitution, Art. IX, Sec. 8 |
| Judges; officials/employees of the superior courts and the OSG | Rule 138, Sec. 35 |
| Government prosecutors | Applying *People v. Villanueva* |
| Civil service officers/employees whose duties require full-time availability | *Ramos v. Rada* (1975) |
| Governors, city and municipal mayors | RA 7160, Sec. 90 |
| Any official barred by special law | — |

## Qualified (practice allowed, within limits)

| Official | What is barred | Legal basis |
|---|---|---|
| Senators, Members of the House | **Personal appearance** as counsel before courts, Electoral Tribunals, quasi-judicial/administrative bodies (includes signing/filing pleadings) | Constitution, Art. VI, Sec. 14 |
| Sanggunian members | Appearing against government units; using government property/personnel except to defend the LGU's own interest | RA 7160, Sec. 91 |
| Government lawyers generally | Practice without the superior's express authority, a stated purpose, and approved leave; any representation adverse to government | CPRA, Canon III, Sec. 21; RA 6713, Sec. 7(b)(2) |

## Retired officials

| Official | Restriction | Legal basis |
|---|---|---|
| Retired Justice/Judge receiving a pension | Cannot appear as counsel against the Government in a civil case, or for an accused government officer/employee in a related criminal case, while receiving the pension | RA 910, Sec. 1 (proviso) — **not** Sec. 5 |

## Bar trap

Governors and mayors are **absolutely** barred; ordinary Sanggunian members are only **qualifiedly** restricted. Do not collapse "local elective official" into one rule — the LGC treats the local chief executive differently from the rest of the Sanggunian.
