# Lawyers Prohibited from Appearing - KP Small Claims IPRA

Exam-facing summary of [[Prohibited Proceedings for Lawyers]]. All three settings share the same shape: a **blanket bar on lawyers appearing as counsel**, plus a **narrow, non-counsel exception**.

| Setting | General rule | Exception | Legal basis |
|---|---|---|---|
| Katarungang Pambarangay | Parties appear in person, without counsel or representative | Lawyer appearing **as a party**; non-lawyer next-of-kin assisting a minor/incompetent | RA 7160, Sec. 415 |
| Small Claims | No lawyer may appear for or represent a party at the hearing | Lawyer appearing **as plaintiff or defendant**; court-allowed non-lawyer assistance if a party cannot properly present the claim/defense | A.M. No. 08-8-7-SC, Secs. 17–18, as amended by OCA Circular No. 69-2022 |
| IPRA customary proceedings | Lawyers prohibited from appearing as counsel for any party | Lawyer appearing as a **Council of Elders/Leaders** member, or by obligation as an IP community member | RA 8371 |

## Bar trap

"As a party" and "as counsel" is the whole exam. A lawyer may always show up **for himself**; what is barred in every row of this table is showing up **for someone else** as counsel. A lawyer who sits silently beside a small-claims litigant is still "appearing... on behalf of" that party within the prohibition.
