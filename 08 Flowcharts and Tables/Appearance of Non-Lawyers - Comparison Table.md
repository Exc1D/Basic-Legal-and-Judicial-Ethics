# Appearance of Non-Lawyers - Comparison Table

Exam-facing summary of [[Appearance of Non-Lawyers]]. Use this table to answer "who may appear without a lawyer, and where" in under thirty seconds.

| Forum / setting | Who may appear | Legal basis | Watch for |
|---|---|---|---|
| MTC / MeTC / MCTC (first-level courts) | Party in person, with an **agent or friend**, or with an attorney | Rule 138, Sec. 34 | The agent-or-friend option **does not** carry over to higher courts |
| RTC and other courts | Party in person, or with a **duly authorized member of the Bar** — no lay assistance | Rule 138, Sec. 34 | Lay "agent or friend" help is no longer allowed once outside the first level |
| Criminal cases (self-defense) | Accused, if the court is satisfied he can properly protect his own rights; court may appoint a resident of good repute where no lawyer is available | Rule 138, Sec. 1(c); Rule 116, Sec. 7 | Not automatic — the court must make the sufficiency finding first |
| Government representation | Any official/person designated by law, whether or not personally a lawyer | Rule 138, Sec. 33 | Full rights of a Bar member, but only for cases where government has an interest |
| NLRC / Labor Arbiters | Non-lawyer representatives | Labor Code, Art. 222 | Tribunal-specific; does not transfer to regular courts |
| DARAB | Non-lawyer representatives; DAR Legal Officer under Chief Legal Division supervision | DARAB Revised Rules of Procedure, Rule VIII, Sec. 44 | Legal Officer's papers must be signed by the Chief Legal Division |
| Cadastral courts | Non-lawyer representing a claimant | Act No. 2259, Sec. 9 | Applies only to cadastral proceedings |
| NCIP | Non-lawyer assistance in proceedings before the NCIP or Regional Hearing Officer | NCIP Administrative Circular No. 1-03 | Specific to indigenous-peoples' proceedings |
| Mediation (ADR Act) | Lawyer or **any other person** designated by a party | RA 9285, Sec. 14 | Waiver of counsel/representation must be in writing; may be rescinded anytime |
| Law student practice | Certified Level 1/2 student, under supervision (Level 2 may appear at trial) | Rule 138-A | Certification is the trigger, not mere enrollment — see [[Law Student Practice Rule]] |

## Bar trap

Do not confuse a forum-specific exception with a general rule. Authorization to appear before the NLRC, DARAB, NCIP, or a Cadastral Court does not extend to regular trial courts, and the first-level-court "agent or friend" rule does not extend upward either.
