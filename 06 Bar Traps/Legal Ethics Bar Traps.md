# Legal Ethics Bar Traps

Use this note for short, exam-facing traps. Each entry should point back to a doctrine note and at least one case.

## Practice of law traps

- Practice of law is not limited to court appearances. See [[Practice of Law]].
- Passing the bar is not enough; oath-taking and signing the Roll matter. See [[Admission to the Bar]] and [[Signing of the Roll of Attorneys]].
- Self-representation is not the same as representing another as counsel. See [[Appearance of Non-Lawyers]].
- Notarial acts are not empty clerical acts. See [[Notarial Practice]].
- A corporation cannot practice law by hiring lawyers as a business front. See [[Unauthorized Practice of Law]].
- A public official who is also a lawyer may be subject to special limits. See [[Public Officials and Private Practice]].
